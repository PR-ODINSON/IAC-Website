ABOUT SECTION:
--------------
Title: Udgam 2026

Intro: The Industry–Academia Conclave (IAC) 2026 is the flagship initiative by the Career Development Centre (CDC), IITRAM, designed to create meaningful connections between students, academia, and industry.

Description: Our vision is to bridge the critical skill gap between classroom learning and real-world industry practices. Through Udgam 2026, we're creating a platform that focuses on innovation, entrepreneurship, employability, and meaningful collaboration across sectors.

This two-day conclave brings together industry leaders, startup founders, students, and faculty to explore emerging trends, share insights, and build partnerships that drive innovation forward.

Core Objectives:
01. Facilitate industry exposure through keynotes and panel discussions
02. Create networking opportunities with industry leaders, HRs, and startup founders
03. Encourage student innovation via idea pitches and project showcases
04. Enable collaborations with ecosystems like GIFT City, MSME, iCreate, and more

FAQ SECTION:
------------
Section: Got Questions?

Title: Frequently Asked Questions

Description: Find answers to common questions about Udgam 2026. Can't find what you're looking for? Feel free to contact us directly.

FAQs:
1. Who can attend the Industry–Academia Conclave 2026?
   Answer: Udgam 2026 is open to all engineering students from colleges across Gujarat. We welcome students from all years and branches who are interested in industry exposure, innovation, and career development.

2. Is there any registration fee for students?
   Answer: No, participation in Udgam 2026 is completely FREE for all students. However, registration is mandatory and seats are limited on a first-come, first-served basis.

3. Will certificates be provided to participants?
   Answer: Yes, all registered participants who attend both days of the event will receive an official participation certificate from CDC, IITRAM. Winners of 'Pitch Your Idea' competition and selected project expo participants will receive special recognition certificates.

4. Can non-IITRAM students participate?
   Answer: Absolutely! Udgam 2026 welcomes students from all engineering colleges across Gujarat. We encourage participation from diverse institutions to benefit from this platform.

5. What should I bring to the event?
   Answer: Bring your student ID card, a notebook for taking notes, and business cards if you have them (for networking). For 'Pitch Your Idea' participants, bring any presentation materials or prototypes you may need.

6. How can companies participate or sponsor the event?
   Answer: Companies can participate through various sponsorship packages (Platinum, Gold, Silver) or book corporate stalls. Please visit our Partners page for detailed packages or contact us directly at cdc@iitram.ac.in for custom partnership opportunities.

7. Can I participate in the 'Pitch Your Idea' competition?
   Answer: Yes! Students with innovative ideas or early-stage startups can register for the pitch competition. Round 1 will be on Day 1 (23 Jan), and finalists will present on Day 2 (24 Jan) in front of investors and startup founders.

8. How do I register for Udgam 2026?
   Answer: Registration will open soon. You can register by visiting our Contact page and filling out the registration form, or by emailing your details to cdc@iitram.ac.in. Follow our official communication channels for registration updates.

ABOUT THE COMPETITION:
---------------------
Section Label: About the Competition

Title: What is "Pitch Your Idea"?

Content:
"Pitch Your Idea" is a flagship innovation competition under Udgam 2026, designed to provide students with a platform to showcase their entrepreneurial spirit and technical ingenuity. This competition brings together aspiring innovators and industry professionals in a dynamic environment where ideas meet opportunity.

Participants will present their startup idea, technical solution, or innovation in front of a distinguished jury comprising industry professionals, successful entrepreneurs, and experienced faculty members. The competition emphasizes real-world problem solving, innovation, and the feasibility of implementation.

The focus is on three key aspects: demonstrating a clear understanding of the problem you're solving, presenting an innovative and practical solution, and communicating your vision effectively. The competition spans two rounds across both days of Udgam 2026, giving participants multiple opportunities to refine and present their ideas.

---

COMPETITION STRUCTURE:
----------------------
Section Label: Competition Format

Title: Two-Round Structure

ROUND 1 - SHORTLISTING ROUND (Day 1):

Format:
- 3–5 minute idea pitch covering problem, solution, target users, and impact
- Optional 1–2 minutes of Q&A, depending on time availability
- Focus on conveying the core concept clearly and concisely

Objective:
Get an overview of as many ideas as possible and identify the most promising concepts for deeper evaluation.

Outcome:
At the end of Round 1, the jury will shortlist 10–15 finalist teams based on predefined criteria.

---

ROUND 2 - FINAL ROUND (Day 2):

Expectations from Finalists:
- Detailed presentation of the idea (problem, solution, unique selling proposition)
- If available: demo video, basic prototype, or wireframes/mockups
- Clear explanation of feasibility, innovation, and potential impact
- Business/technical model and implementation roadmap
- Extended Q&A session with the jury

Evaluation:
Finalists will be evaluated more deeply across all criteria, and winners will be chosen based on their performance in this round.

Note: Finalists will be informed about their selection and time slots for Round 2 at the end of Day 1.

---

EVALUATION CRITERIA:
--------------------
Section Label: How You'll Be Judged

Title: Evaluation Criteria

Intro: Round 1 uses these criteria in a quick, high-level manner, while Round 2 applies them in greater depth with more weightage on each aspect.

Criteria:
1. Problem Understanding
   - Clarity in identifying and explaining the real-world problem.

2. Innovation & Creativity
   - How unique, original, or innovative the solution is.

3. Feasibility & Implementation
   - Practicality of execution, technical feasibility, and scalability.

4. Impact & Value Proposition
   - Who benefits, what changes, and how significant the impact could be.

5. Presentation & Communication
   - Structure of the pitch, clarity of explanation, and ability to answer questions.

---

WHAT YOU NEED TO PREPARE:
-------------------------
Section Label: Preparation Guide

Title: What You Need to Prepare

ROUND 1 - Initial Pitch:
- Short pitch (3–5 minutes)
- Basic explanation of problem, proposed solution, and target beneficiaries
- Optional: Basic slide deck (3–5 slides)

ROUND 2 - Final Presentation (Finalists):
- Refined pitch deck (up to 8–10 slides)
- If available: Prototype, demo video, or UI mockups/architecture diagrams
- Detailed implementation approach and business/technical model
- Roadmap and next steps

Note: Prototypes are encouraged but not mandatory – a strong, well-thought-out idea can still win.
